The
Black National
Anthem

Lift every voice and sing
Till earth and heaven ring,
Ring with the harmonies of Liberty;
Let our rejoicing rise
High as the listening skies,
Let it resound loud as the rolling sea.
Sing a song full of the faith that the dark past has taught us,
Sing a song full of the hope that the present has brought us,
Facing the rising sun of our new day begun
Let us march on till victory is won.

How & When

The "Black National Anthum" was first performed publicly by a choir of 500 schoolchildren at the segregated Stanton School in Jacksonville, Florida, to celebrate President Abraham Lincoln’s birthday. Over time, it became a powerful symbol of hope, unity, and resilience for African Americans, especially during the Civil Rights Movement of the 1950s and 1960s.

THE
LYRICAL GENIUS
BEHIND:
"Lift Every Voice and Sing"

“Lift Every Voice and Sing” stands out as a lyrical masterpiece due to its profound and poignant verses that encapsulate the historical struggles and triumphs of African Americans.
The lyrics, penned by James Weldon Johnson in 1900, eloquently convey the journey from slavery to freedom, touching on themes of faith, hope, resilience, and unity.
Each line is imbued with powerful imagery and emotion, reflecting the collective experience of a marginalized community striving for equality and justice.
Through its evocative language and timeless message, the hymn has become a symbol of strength and perseverance for generations, earning its place as a cultural treasure in American history.

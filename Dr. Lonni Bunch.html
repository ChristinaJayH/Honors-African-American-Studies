Dr. Lonnie Bunch

The National African American Museum

Lonnie G. Bunch III, the first director of the National Museum of African American History and Culture, turned a dream into a reality by leading the museum’s growth from scratch with no team, money, or location to becoming a major cultural spot in New York. Through his leadership and commitment, he obtained a collection of 40,000 objects that tell the story of African American's impact on American History. The museum, which opened in September 2016, has welcomed over 8 million visitors and stands as the nation’s largest and most comprehensive center dedicated to exploring and showcasing the African American experience.

The Cultural Signifigance of the National African American Museum

The National Museum of African American History and Culture holds immense cultural significance as it serves as a dedicated space to document and showcase the rich history, contributions, and experiences of African Americans in the United States. By providing a platform for interactive exhibitions, the museum allows visitors to explore and appreciate African American culture, fostering a deeper understanding of its impact on American society. Through its artifacts, artworks, and exhibitions, the museum highlights the resilience, optimism, and spirituality inherent in African American history, reflecting core American values.

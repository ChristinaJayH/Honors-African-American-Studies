Faith Ringgold

Overview

Faith Ringgold was born on October 8, 1930 in New York City’s Harlem district. She grew up with a passion for art. While still in high school, she decided to pursue a career as an artist. Despite facing racism and segregation, Ringgold’s family provided a loving environment that nurtured her artistic talents. She explored visual art from a young age through sewing and fabric work from her mother.

Quilts and the African American Community

Many families and peple of the African American Community can say they've got a quilt. Family hierloooms being passed down from mother to daughter, and so on. The concept of quilts had originated all the way back to the time of slavery. Slaveowners would often force enslaved Africans to sew them quilts, they were made to be the best of the best. Often times in the tragedy and saddness of slavery, they would use the scraps of the fabric used for the quilts and make their own. It was even seen as a community activity, to get away from the throughts of slavery and oppresion. These quilts have been passde down through generations.
